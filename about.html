About E-Waste

Learn what e-waste is and why tackling it is essential for our future.

What is E-Waste?

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices such as smartphones, computers, refrigerators, and televisions. Rapid technological advancement means devices become obsolete quickly, fueling a global surge in e-waste.

Why is E-Waste a Growing Crisis?

  • Frequent Upgrades: Consumer demand and innovation drive people to replace devices more often.
  • Improper Disposal: Many electronics are not disposed of correctly. E-waste in landfills releases hazardous substances like lead and mercury into the environment.
  • Low Recycling Rates: Globally, only a small fraction of e-waste is recycled properly, increasing ecological and health risks.
Did you know? Over 50 million metric tons of e-waste are generated each year globally, but only about 17% is formally recycled.

Why Should You Care?

  • Environmental Pollution: Toxic chemicals from e-waste contaminate soil and water, harming wildlife and ecosystems.
  • Health Risks: Exposure to hazardous materials in e-waste can cause respiratory issues, neurological damage, and other serious health problems, especially for communities near informal recycling sites.
  • Sustainability: Raising awareness and promoting responsible recycling and reuse helps reduce the negative impact of e-waste and supports a more sustainable future.
