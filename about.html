About E-Waste

Learn what e-waste is and why tackling it is essential for our future.

What is E-Waste?

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices such as smartphones, computers, refrigerators, and televisions. With rapid technological advancements, devices become outdated quickly, contributing to a global surge in e-waste.

Why is E-Waste a Growing Crisis?

Constant innovation and consumer demand result in frequent upgrades and increased disposal of electronic devices. Improper disposal releases toxic chemicals like lead and mercury into the environment. Most e-waste isn’t recycled properly, posing significant ecological and health risks.

Why Should You Care?

E-waste contaminates soil and water, harms wildlife, and endangers human health. By raising awareness and promoting responsible recycling and reuse, we can minimize its negative impact and move toward a more sustainable world.
